Contorg: The Content Organizing App

What is Contorg?

Organizing, tracking, and gaining insight into the things we choose to consume such music, books, video games, film, televison and more, today is an incredibly difficult task. A task this website seeks to solve in part. With the explosion of content, and the multi-service nature of that content, it can be difficult to track your material content in a centralized resource. If one wanted to track the content they consumed, it would be very complex. Content providers such as amazon(kindle and prime), netflix, hulu, HBO, Spotify, Apple Music, and more continue to proliferate while mass-content trackers such as imdb or goodreads, and these services often do not allow more custom unverified inputs. And switching between difference services for tracking personal content may be quite annoying. Particularly if you choose to track information like the page number you were on, or personal ratings scales, and more.

Some of the issues are unfortunately structural, most of these companies value their(read: your) data highly, and will not grant access to it publicly via standardized manners called APIs, leaving us in a situation where it is incredibly difficult or impossible to coalesce this data into one source such as my website automatically with ease. And even knowing which for example films or TV shows are available on which streaming service is non-trivial, as you need to have access to a trustworthy database of such knowledge which is generally not free and therefore not an option for a website like ours. On the other hand manual scrapping can be difficult to tune since much of the information is unverified and unsure.
